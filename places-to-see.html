Forests & Woodlands

Carrick Wood

Well worth exploring, there is a folly, known locally as the Spire, set on a high point within the wood. It was built on top of an earlier windmill by the Viscount Carlow in order to give employment during a hard winter.

Website
Durrow Abbey

Part of an old woodland estate. Species remaining on site are Scots pine and Norway spruce and some birch and rowan. Other flora include rushes, grasses, bracken and meadowsweet. Fauna include pheasant, woodcock, grey squirrel and mink.

Website
Garryhinch

Located in a typical woodland setting, this site is part of the old Warburton Estate. The woodlands lie on both sides of the river Barrow. In this pleasant woodland landscape, you may also observe fauna such as fallow deer, grey and red squirrels, rabbits and a broad variety of birdlife.

Visitor Centres

Tullamore D.E.W.

Visit the home of Tullamore D.E.W. and join the the immersive sensory journey which brings the stories of Irish Whiskey, the distilling process and Tullamore D.E.W. to life.

From summer 2021, the new visitor centre will be located at the actual Tullamore D.E.W. distillery.

Website
Corlea Trackway

Hidden away in the boglands of Longford, not far from Kenagh village, is an inspiring relic of prehistory: a togher – an Iron Age road – built in 148 BC. Known locally as the Danes’ Road, it is the largest of its kind to have been uncovered in Europe.

Inside the interpretive centre, an 18-metre stretch of the ancient wooden structure is on permanent display in a hall specially designed to preserve it. Don’t miss this amazing remnant of our ancient past.

Website
Athlone Castle

Trace the footprints of the generations who shaped this place. From early settlements and warring chieftains to foreign invaders and local heroes. This site on the River Shannon is the centre of Ireland’s Hidden Heartlands.
